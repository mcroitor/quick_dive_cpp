# ISO 690:2022 Quick Reference

This guide supports citation and bibliography normalization for the Academic Manuscript Editor skill.

## Scope and Policy
- Default citation and bibliography standard: ISO 690:2022.
- Do not mix citation styles within one manuscript.
- If target venue rules require another style, follow venue rules and mark the override explicitly.

## Citation Systems
Use one system consistently across the manuscript:
- Author-date system.
- Numeric system.

If the task does not specify a system, prefer author-date unless the venue requires numeric references.

## Core Bibliography Rules
- Keep author names and source titles faithful to source metadata.
- Preserve year, edition, publisher, and place when available.
- Include DOI when available.
- For online sources, include URL and access date when context requires.
- Apply consistent punctuation and ordering across all entries.

## Templates (Generic)
Use these templates as normalization targets. Adapt punctuation consistently within the selected citation system.

### Book
- Author, A. Title. Edition. Place: Publisher, Year. ISBN.

### Journal Article
- Author, A. Article title. Journal title, Year, Volume(Issue), pages. DOI.

### Chapter in Edited Book
- Author, A. Chapter title. In: Editor, E. (ed.). Book title. Place: Publisher, Year, pages. ISBN.

### Conference Paper
- Author, A. Paper title. In: Conference title, place, date. Place: Publisher, Year, pages. DOI/URL.

### Thesis or Dissertation
- Author, A. Title. Thesis type. Institution, Place, Year.

### Web Resource
- Author or Organization. Title [online]. Year or date. Available from: URL [Accessed DD Mon YYYY].

## In-Text Citation Guidance
- Author-date: keep author spelling and year consistent with bibliography entries.
- Numeric: keep numbering stable and ordered by first appearance.
- Every in-text citation must map to one bibliography entry.
- Every bibliography entry should be cited in text unless explicitly allowed otherwise.

## Validation Checklist
- Exactly one citation system is used.
- All in-text citations have matching bibliography entries.
- No uncited bibliography entries unless intentionally included and marked.
- Required fields are present by source type.
- DOI, URL, and access-date usage is consistent.
- Duplicate and near-duplicate entries are reconciled.

## Common Errors
- Mixing numeric and author-date styles.
- Missing year or publisher in book references.
- Missing issue or pages in journal references when available.
- Omitting DOI for sources that have one.
- Inconsistent access-date formats.
